Lechon is a very popular Filipino dish of a whole pig roasted over an open fire until the skin is crispy and golden. The meat becomes tender and flavorful when seasoned with aromatics like lemongrass, garlic, and spices. It’s a centerpiece at Filipino celebrations, especially in Cebu, and is famous for its distinctive, savory lechon style.
  • Cebu
Sisig is a popular Filipino dish made from chopped pork, typically including parts like the pig’s face and ears, seasoned with onions, chili, and calamansi juice. It’s often served sizzling on a hot plate, giving it a crispy texture with savory, tangy, and spicy flavors. Originating from Pampanga, sisig is now a staple across the Philippines, enjoyed as a main dish and a pulutan (bar snack).
  • Pampanga
Lumpia is a Filipino spring roll filled with a mix of vegetables, ground meat, or seafood, wrapped in a thin pastry and fried until crisp and golden brown. It was inspired by Chinese spring rolls and adapted to Filipino tastes, with Lumpiang Shanghai being one of the most popular versions, typically filled with seasoned ground pork.
  • Luzon
Bulalo is a Filipino beef soup made with beef shanks and marrow bones, resulting in a rich, flavorful broth. The dish is typically simmered with cabbage, corn, potatoes, and green beans, enhancing its taste. Originating from Batangas, bulalo is especially popular in cooler regions and is loved for its warm, hearty, and nourishing qualities.
  • Batangas
Bicol Express is a Filipino dish made with pork cooked in a rich, spicy coconut milk sauce with shrimp paste and chili peppers, offering a creamy and fiery flavor.
  • Bicol
